Welcome to the Firefox Private Network Beta

Thanks for participating in this limited-time, free beta of Firefox Private Network. Firefox Private Network lets you use public Wi-Fi more safely and securely. It does this by encrypting the web addresses you visit and the data you send to most websites. This helps protect your web browsing from hackers, websites, and trackers.

Your privacy

Firefox cares about your privacy. Firefox Private Network doesn’t collect information about your browsing. We do collect generic information about how you use the extension, such as data about how you interact with it, when you turn it off, and technical information about how it functions. Our privacy policy describes how we handle this data. You may opt out of sharing this data with us at anytime.

How to opt-out of sending performance data

  1. Click the menu button and choose OptionsPreferences.

  2. Select the Privacy & Security panel.

  3. Scroll down to the Firefox Data Collection and Use section.

  4. Uncheck Allow Firefox to send technical and interaction data to Mozilla.

Using Private Network

Look for the Private Network icon in your Firefox toolbar.

Select the icon and you will see a message asking you to sign in with a Firefox account.

Once you are signed in, you will see a green check, the Private Network is on. Use Firefox as usual, and your online activity will be encrypted and sent through a proxy service provided by our partner Cloudflare.

To turn off Private Network just click the icon, and toggle the on/off switch in the panel that pops up. When Private Network is off, your Firefox activity will route directly to the network you are connected to and will not be encrypted or sent through the Private Network Proxy Service.

Giving feedback and getting updates

Because this is a beta test, Firefox Private Network will occasionally open a tab in Firefox to give you updates or to ask for your feedback about your experience.

If you want to share your thoughts at any time, just click the Firefox Private Network icon, then the settings menu . From there you’ll see a link to Give us feedback. We’d love to hear from you.

If at any time you need help with the Private Network, you can access support from the menu by clicking the Help & support link.
